Nux Vomica

I am the greatest of polychrests

Poison-nut

When this remedy is indicated in influenza, the person may have have high fever, violent chills, strong nausea and cramping in the digestive tract (or a painful cough and constricted breathing if the flu is respiratory). Headache usually occurs, along with oversensitivity to sound, bright light, and odors. A person who needs Nux vomica is often very irritable, feeling worse from exertion and worse from being cold in any way.

Nose.-Stuffed up, at night especially. Stuffy colds, snuffles, after exposure to dry, cold atmosphere; worse, in warm room. Odors tend to produce fainting. Coryza: fluent in daytime; stuffed up at night and outdoors; or alternates between nostrils. Bleeding in morning (Bry). Acrid discharge, but with stuffed up feeling.


Throat.--Rough, scraped feeling. Tickling after waking in morning. Sensation of roughness, tightness, and tension. Pharynx constricted. Uvula swollen. Stitches into ear.

Tongue-Tongue covered with a (heavy) white, thick, or yellowish coating, or tongue dry, cracked (on edges), brownish or blackish, with bright red margins. Great heaviness of tongue, with difficulty of speech, and sensation when speaking, as if tongue had become thicker. Stuttering. Lisping. Sour taste in mouth, sour odor of breath. Throat.


Respiratory.--Catarrhal hoarseness, with scraping in throat. Spasmodic constriction. Asthma. Cough, with sensation as if something were torn loose in chest. Shallow respiration. Oppressed breathing. Tight, dry hacking cough; at times with bloody expectoration. Cough brings on bursting headache and bruised pain in epigastric region
